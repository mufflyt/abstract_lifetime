Thanks for helping with the AAGL 2023 abstract-to-publication project. We're tracking which of the 98 abstracts from the AAGL 2023 supplement (JMIG vol. 30, issue 11, Supplement S) — **60 Oral Presentations** and **38 Video Presentations** — went on to be published as full papers. Our pipeline auto-decides the easy cases; we need human eyes on the **20 ambiguous ones**.

### App

**https://mufflyt.shinyapps.io/aagl-adjudication/**

It may take 10–15 seconds to load the first time (cold start). Bookmark the URL.

### What you'll do

1. **Enter your initials** in the "Reviewer Initials" box (top of the right panel). Use 2–4 uppercase letters — e.g., `GT` for Gabby, `MS` for Megan. Your initials get stamped on every decision so we can tell reviewers apart and flag disagreements.
2. On the left sidebar, set **"Algorithm classification"** to **Review** — that narrows the list from 98 down to the 20 abstracts that need human judgment.
3. For each abstract:
   - Read the **title, authors, and abstract text** on the left.
   - The right panel shows **PubMed candidates** the algorithm thinks might be the published version. They're sorted by score (highest first).
   - **Click a candidate row** to expand that paper's abstract for a side-by-side comparison.
   - Choose one:
     - **Confirmed match** — one of the candidates is the published paper. If it's not the top one, paste its PMID into **"Override PMID"**.
     - **No match found** — none of the candidates is the right paper (either it wasn't published, or it's not in PubMed yet).
     - **Skip / Unsure** — come back later.
   - Optional: add a note in **"Reviewer notes"** if something's worth recording (e.g., "same first author but different study").
   - Click **Save Decision** (or hit `Enter`).

### Keyboard shortcuts

- `m` = match, `n` = no match, `s` = skip
- `Enter` = save
- `←` / `→` (or `[` / `]`) = prev / next abstract

These only fire when you're not typing in a text box.

### Good to know

- **You'll see `[A]` / `[R]` / `[x]` / `[0]` prefixes** in the dropdown. These are the algorithm's pre-decisions (accept / review / reject / no candidates). You're primarily working the `[R]` ones, but if something in `[A]` or `[x]` looks wrong while browsing, feel free to override.
- **Conflicts**: if one of you already decided an abstract and the other reaches the same one, the app will warn you. We expect some disagreements — that's the point of having two reviewers. Just record your own decision; we'll resolve conflicts at the end.
- **Auto-filled rows**: you'll see 78 rows already in the sheet tagged `reviewer = AUTO` — those are the algorithm's own decisions on unambiguous cases. You don't need to re-review them unless something catches your eye.
- **Everything saves to Google Sheets automatically** — there's no "submit all" step. Close the tab whenever. Come back anytime.
